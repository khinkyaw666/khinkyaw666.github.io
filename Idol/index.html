Earily Life and Education

Leonardo DiCaprio was born on November 11, 1974, in Los Angeles to George and Irmelin DiCaprio. He grew up in a creative and bohemian environment, with his parents divorcing when he was young. DiCaprio’s acting career began at a young age, with his first notable role in the TV series Parenthood in 1990. He attended Los Angeles Center for Enriched Studies and later Le Lycée Français de New York, a bilingual school where he became fluent in French. Despite his education, his passion for acting led him to drop out of high school at 16 to pursue a career in film. His early roles in television and movies set him on the path to becoming one of Hollywood's most famous actors.

Leonardo DiCaprio’s career began in the early 1990s, with his breakout role in Titanic (1997), which made him an international star. He worked with top directors like Martin Scorsese, starring in films such as Gangs of New York (2002) and The Aviator (2004). DiCaprio earned critical acclaim for his roles in Inception (2010) and The Wolf of Wall Street (2013), and won an Oscar for The Revenant (2015). Beyond acting, he is known for his environmental activism, supporting causes related to climate change.
